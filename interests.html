My interests

I have found interest in movies since I was 14 years old like most people, and at some point in our lives, the majority of us would have felt as though we had hit rock bottom. There's nothing wrong with admitting it, as acceptance is the first step to seeing things as they are and moving forward to better ourselves.
The alternative to moving forward can vary from one person to another. It could be a meaningful book for one, a motivational song for another, or an uplifting movie for someone else.
So here is Top 5 movies you should watch that changed my perspective on life!

1.Soul

When jazz pianist Joe lands the gig of a lifetime, things don't go according to plan when he ends up trapped between Earth and the afterlife and has to learn what it means to have a soul.
Soul brings up deep questions about death, appreciating life, what passions mean in life, and more.
It taught me about how we define success in our society and the pressures that come with it. and it also taught me how to redefine what I consider "success" to be on my own terms.

2.The Shawshank Redemption

This film will fuel your hopes and motivate you to pursue your dreams.The Shawshank Redemption is a story about enduring the pain, trauma, and torture that the protagonists, Andy (Tim Robbins) and Red (Morgan Freeman) while at the Shawshank penitentiary.
These two characters are put through so many trials and turbulence and what keeps them alive is their hope to one day be free. There are so many powerful messages within this film but one that will hit you hard is that perseverance will be rewarded. Andy's 19 years of plotting and planning his prison escape led him to his freedom. Even if these 19 years meant so many traumatic episodes, he holds on to his hope through it all, which ultimately has a positive impact on Red.

3.Forrest Gump

One of the best performances by Tom Hanks, who takes over the role of the protagonist, Forrest Gump it's a film that focuses on the importance of hope, in turn, making you realize how precious every life is. The protagonist, Forrest, endures many prejudices due to his "different" nature and his low IQ.
However, Forrest proves that anyone is capable of being who they want to be as long as they are prepared to try new things, pursue their passions, and stay optimistic throughout. Forrest's story is no simple one but at the end, he shows how his beautiful heart and mind allowed him to attain the love and desire he needed.

4.The Intouchables

The Intouchables is a well-known French comedy-drama. The plot of this film revolves around an aristocrat named Philippe who becomes a quadriplegic after an unfortunate paragliding accident.
Later, he employs a young man named Driss as his caregiver.The film explores the real needs of people in life, respect and understanding, acceptance and love.
And this movie explores the themes of friendship, adventure, and love in a comedic manner, and is bound to leave you amused in the end.

5.The Pursuit of Happyness

An autobiographical film that gives you a glimpse of the struggles Chris Gardner went through. With Will Smith taking over Gardner's role and Smith's son, Jaden Smith, taking over Gardner's son's role, The Pursuit of Happyness is all about enduring, having hope, and achieving your dreams. If you are at a point in life where you feel like giving up or are losing hope, then this is the film to watch.
This particular film shows how Gardner struggled with homelessness while raising his toddler son in the 1980s. This struggling man goes through many obstacles including issues with his wife, inability to afford basic needs, and trying to fulfill his son's needs.
